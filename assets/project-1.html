Data Analysis of How Top Tennis Players' Performance Evolves

ITCS-3162: Introduction to Data Mining, Project 1

Andrew Morgan

In a sea of popular team sports, tennis has differentiated itself as perhaps the most played and one of the most watched individual sports. All your errors are your own, with no teammates to blame, and how your individual performance changes over time is the only relevant statistic. At the top of the game, the margin for error in individual performance becomes razor-thin; what might match-by-match performance data tell us about how Novak Djokovic or Jannik Sinner subtly adjust and improve their game to try and beat those margins? The goal of this project is to identify trends in top tennis players' performance data and make data-driven inferences about what how they changed their games over time (or failed to do so). We are also interested in comparing the evolutions of various top tennis players' games.

I use Jeff Sackman's publicly available ATP match dataset: https://github.com/JeffSackmann/tennis_atp.

Inspired by a desire to expand on this video on Djokovic's performance evolution from ESPN: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=s200n5ejiFc

def plot_data(df, xlabel, ylabel, invert):
    # setting up the plot
    sns.set(font="monospace", rc={"figure.figsize":(18,18)})
    plot = sns.lineplot(x=df["x"], y=df["y"])
    plot.set_xlabel(xlabel, fontsize=20)
    plot.set_ylabel(ylabel, fontsize=20)

    if invert:
        plot.invert_yaxis()
    
    # rounding the x ticks 
    # https://stackoverflow.com/questions/53747298/how-to-format-axis-tick-labels-from-number-to-thousands-or-millions-125-436-to
    plt.gca().xaxis.set_major_formatter(ticker.FuncFormatter(lambda tick, pos: "{:.0f}".format(tick)[:4]))
    
    plt.show()

We see from this chart that Federer seems to have gone bigger or faster on his serve as his career progressed; his early professional career had him serving an ace about 8% of his service points, while the peak of his career had him serving at nearly 12%. This could be a symptom of growing comfort with his serve and perhaps greater confidence in his ability as he rose the rankings from 2000 onward. We can also take a look here at Federer's serving consistency, with the confidence interval (shaded area in the chart) of his ace rate for the years 2010 to 2015 being only about 1% (with consistency much lower at the very beginning and end of his career). However, the trend in his rank goes against the upward trend in his ace rate, so maybe his ace rate has less to do with his succes than other metrics.
